distributive-cube sequence
[Function]

Arguments and Values:
sequence            an integer list.

Description:

This is a function at the heart of the OPUSMODUS realisations of interference techniques
found in the Schillinger System of Music Composition. This system, popular in the USA in
the mid 20C and studied by Gershwin and many lm composers, has been reassessed in
recent years by Dr Jeremy Arden.

The DISTRIBUTIVE-CUBE is identical to the DISTRIBUTIVE-SQUARE except that its
results are more voluminous and have lengths related to the cube of the original. A sub-group
of 1 bar of 4/4 would produce a bar group and resultant rhythm of 64 bars.

(distributive-cube '(3 1))
=> ((27 9 9 3) (9 3 3 1))

Examples:
(setf dc-i2 (distributive-cube (interference2 '(3 2))))
=> ((8 4 4 8 4 2 2 4 4 2 2 4 8 4 4 8)
    (4 2 2 4 2 1 1 2 2 1 1 2 4 2 2 4)
    (4 2 2 4 2 1 1 2 2 1 1 2 4 2 2 4)
    (8 4 4 8 4 2 2 4 4 2 2 4 8 4 4 8))
